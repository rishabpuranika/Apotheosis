Chapter 3209 An Outsider



A sharp, snapping sound cracked through the air.

A purple ribbon lashed out at Luo Zheng, sending him flying with the force of the blow.

Concubine Yan rushed up to Feng Ge and held her in her arms, looking worried.

Luo Zheng steadied himself midair and landed cleanly on the ground. He stood up unharmed, giving Feng Ge a strange look.

In theory, as soon as the demonic eye stopped releasing the power of fear, then the imposed fears of the opponent would vanish.

Luo Zheng had released the power of his demonic eye to frighten her, but even now that he had stopped, Feng Ge remained in a state of deep fear. She seemed unable to shake off her terror.

The divine flames of the Jin Wu were gradually burning themselves out. Soon they extinguished, surrounded by the black, charred remnants of everything burned by the flames.

In the arms of Concubine Yan, Feng Ge still trembled and shuddered with fear.

Concubine Yan raised her hand and gently touched the spot between Feng Ge's brows. A soothing aura passed through her fingers and flowed into Feng Ge's body, until she began to calm down.

Concubine Yan turned to Luo Zheng. "Did you use some trick to cause her such fear?" she demanded.

The Truth of Goodwill and Malice was a rather unpopular Truth of Divine Dao in the Taiyi Heaven Palace, but Concubine Yan still recognized it immediately. What she didn't know was that Luo Zheng had used the power of the demonic eye.

"Yes," said Luo Zheng.

Concubine Yan sighed. Instead of blaming Luo Zheng, she said guiltily, "It's my fault...

After she was born, she was put through a series of very cruel tests in the Divine Lair. Your trick has brought back those terrible experiences to her."

Many people imagined that a demon woman of the Divine Lair would grow up in an environment of love and care just like humans. In truth, it was nothing like that.

Feng Ge, for instance, had been considered a front-runner in the quest for the Divine Lair's next leader from the moment she was born.

To establish her right to become the leader, she had been forced to compete from a very early age with other demon women of the Divine Lair in fights to the death. Countless young girls like her died at her hands, while she herself sustained various injuries and traumas. She grew up exposed to cruelty, death, and suffering. When Feng Ge was six years old, Eastern Emperor Taiyi fought his way into the Divine Lair and took her under his care. If not for his intervention, Feng Ge would have become the leader already.

Although Feng Ge spent the next several years in the relative safety of the Phoenix Palace, the memory of her childhood was indelible. Concubine Yan had needed to use a special method to seal Feng Ge's traumatic memories in the depths of her soul.

However, there were some theurgies of the Truth of Goodwill and Malice that could still trigger her and stir up her childhood memories.

Feng Ge's bouts with panic and fear were particularly unsettling because she had otherwise developed a facade of indifference and detachment. It was the only way she could survive the brutal experiences of her childhood.

"Then why didn't you stop us just now?" Luo Zheng admonished, frowning.

Luo Zheng hadn't expected that he would have to fight Feng Ge with Concubine Yan on the sidelines, watching the battle the entire time.

He felt anger rising in him. He had needed to force Feng Ge to release Liusu, Nelly and Xun.

Concubine Yan continued to rub the spot between Feng Ge's eyebrows, sadness appearing in her face. "I made a mistake. I wanted to see how far she had come, by seeing how far you could push her..."

Upon her return to the Phoenix Palace, Feng Ge had been mostly laconic and indifferent to everything going on around her. Concubine Yan spent as much time with her as she could, often staying by her side throughout the day, but there seemed to be little to no improvement in Feng Ge's disposition. As a mother, she couldn't help but feel desperate for anything that could shake her daughter out of her apathy.

But Concubine Yan's efforts were in vain. Nothing she did succeeded in changing her daughter's attitude. Feng Ge's heart had been shaped by fear and cruelty, and she now used indifference as a wall to guard against everything and anything that could do further damage to her psyche.

"Wouldn't it be more painful for both of you if I kept pushing her?" Luo Zheng asked with a sigh.

Concubine Yan gave him a small smile. "No. When she is with you, she seems to be feeling things more deeply. Her emotions are richer. I am her mother and I never saw her that angry, but today I even saw her cry..."

When Concubine Yan first brought Luo Zheng to her, Feng Ge was indeed indifferent to her mother. She was apathetic towards everyone, really.

Luo Zheng wanted to roll his eyes, although he resisted the urge. This was too strange for him. He couldn't help feeling like this Concubine Yan and Feng Ge were a matched pair of lunatics. Should he scare Feng Ge occasionally to satisfy Concubine Yan?

"The Soul Fire Swirl is not a punishment. It's actually a cultivation method.

Even the direct line of the Phoenix Palace would cherish this kind of cultivation method. The people you want to find should not be in danger,"

Concubine Yan said.

Luo Zheng was stunned for a moment. To himself he thought, with a trace of exasperation, 'If you two had made that clear earlier, we could have avoided the fight entirely.'

After a while, Feng Ge finally awoke, and she found herself lying in her mother's arms.

She saw Luo Zheng nearby and glared at him. Breaking free from Concubine Yan's arms, she hurriedly got up and started to leave the place.

"You lost to me," Luo Zheng said behind her.

Feng Ge stopped in her track and asked coldly, "So what do you want?"

"Take me to find them," Luo Zheng said calmly.

Concubine Yan watched their exchange without comment. Feng Ge hesitated, looking torn, then she seemed to make up her mind. "Follow me," she told Luo Zheng.

Luo Zheng glanced at Concubine Yan, who nodded her assent. Feng Ge marched out and Luo Zheng followed her as she made her way to the Fire Bearing Hall.

Concubine Yan watched them leave, gazing at Luo Zheng's tall figure from behind. She smiled to herself, and murmured, "He's not afraid of the divine flames of the Jin Wu, and his strength is greater than Feng Ge's. I think they could be a good match..."

Streams of hot, intense auras reached Feng Ge and Luo Zheng as they drew closer to the Fire Bearing Hall.

The walls of the hall were strung with glowing, red-hot chains, which drooped down like noodles as the metal of the chains softened in the burning heat. When Feng Ge and Luo Zheng glanced up at the beams supporting the chains, they could see the tongues of flame licking at the pillars and eaves.

When they looked down at the floor, which was also emanating heat, they could see the same burning flames in the cracks.

Luo Zheng was curious and would have liked to hear what people were cultivating in the Fire Bearing Hall. But, catching sight of Feng Ge's stony, sullen face, he decided it would be best to stay quiet for now.

The two of them walked silently down a long, narrow corridor, then Feng Ge abruptly turned and entered a side hall. Luo Zheng followed.

Luo Zheng instantly sensed a soul aura within the scorching, intense heat of the side hall.

His eyes lit up with excitement. This aura was quite similar to the aura of the soul pills. It was similar to that of the starlight that had landed outside Dragon City. And yet, the aura here was far stronger than either of those.

Indeed, the aura here was at least ten times stronger than that of the soul pills or the starlight.

There were three people seated cross-legged in the center of the hall.

Xun, Nelly, and Liusu sat beneath several red-hot chains. There were small swirls of flame burning under each chain, and these seemed to be the source of the powerful soul aura.

It was the first time Luo Zheng had seen a soul cultivated in such a manner. He was surprised and interested.

"Is this the Soul Fire Swirl?" he asked.

"Well, what else would it be?" Feng Ge snapped, still angry with Luo Zheng.

Luo Zheng looked mildly embarrassed. "Ah, I thought it was a kind of torture for a moment..."

Feng Ge's indifference was giving way to anger as she stared fiercely at Luo Zheng.

At that moment, Nelly opened her eyes, and the flame swirling over her head immediately dissipated along the chain above her. Nelly saw Feng Ge and grinned. "Little Master, I feel my aura has improved again! Eh? Luo Zheng?"

Luo Zheng, watching Nelly, was puzzled by her reaction.

He had not expected her to treat Feng Ge as though she was an intimate, and he was surprised to feel like somewhat of an outsider.

Liusu and Xun also opened their eyes. When Liusu saw Luo Zheng, she showed no sign of distress or worry. She only smiled sweetly and said,

"You finally found us here!"

"Little Master said that you probably wouldn't be able to enter the Phoenix Palace," Xun said, also favoring Luo Zheng with a smile.

Luo Zheng was nonplussed. He had fought so hard to find these three, but now they were acting as though they hadn't been in danger at all. He realized that they had not been imprisoned. They were never in any kind of peril, and they had not been worried about anything.
