Chapter 3210 Strangers

← Prev Index Next →


Luo Zheng stood there uncertainly and frowned. "You called her Little Master?"

Xun, Nelly and Liusu exchanged knowing glances, then they all turned to face Luo Zheng. "We have all become Feng Ge's disciples!" Nelly beamed. "Since Feng Ge is still quite young, we address her as 'Little Master'."

Luo Zheng shot a glance at Feng Ge, who stared back at him unflinchingly.

"But since you are fine, let's go back already," Luo Zheng said.

He figured it wasn't appropriate for his wives to stay in the Phoenix Palace.

He couldn't fathom why they needed to become disciples of Feng Ge.

However, to his surprise, both Nelly and Liusu shook their heads. Even Xun smiled wistfully without saying a word.

Luo Zheng knew Xun wouldn't ever go against his wishes, but he could tell from her reaction that she also thought the Phoenix Palace would be an excellent place to cultivate.

He scratched his head, trying to figure out what to do.

Initially, the girls had been against the idea. However, since Feng Ge didn't cause them trouble and instead treated them as those direct descendants sincerely, they warmed up to the idea of training under her.

They considered their options. The Phoenix Palace was definitely superior compared to the environment outside the Dragon City. Even the Heart Flow Sect couldn't come close to it. After all, it was built by Eastern Emperor Taiyi; of course its resources were of the best quality.

Besides, Feng Ge did give them advice from time to time. No one could deny her talent. With her input and their good understandings, they made notable progress more rapidly than before.

As a result, the three girls started calling her Little Master...

Seeing Luo Zheng's upset expression, Liusu came forward and held his hand in hers. "It's safe here." She batted her eyelashes at him. "If we were in the Dragon City, we might not be able to reach the Other Shore in decades. We can save a lot of time here!"

There were many challenges on the way to the Other Shore, such as the nine lines and the huge whirlpools on the Sea of Truth, which posed a great risk. No one knew how many people were lost to the whirlpools or died on the nine lines. However, this risk could be greatly reduced if they could train in the Phoenix Palace.

Luo Zheng sighed. He knew this was true.

Even if he had earned a lot of divine crystals by virtue of his Instant Enlightenment, he would not be able to provide such a good environment for the girls.

But seeing Feng Ge gloating over him, he couldn't help but feel exasperated.

"Why did you bring them here?" Luo Zheng suddenly asked her.

Feng Ge hesitated for a while. "I want to get the Truth Enlightenment."

On the day of the Seven Mountains Meeting, Feng Ge had been severely humiliated by Luo Zheng since he had more words of the Truth Enlightenment than she did.

She lost to Luo Zheng not because she was not powerful enough, but because her understanding of the Truth Enlightenment was inferior to Luo Zheng.

This fed her hunger to grow stronger, but naturally, her pride didn't let her ask Luo Zheng for the words directly.

Thus, when she found out that Luo Zheng had family cultivating in the Dragon City, she arranged to have them brought to the Phoenix Palace.

Her initial plan was to blackmail Luo Zheng into handing over his Truth Enlightenment by holding these women hostage.

On the other hand, Nelly, Xun and Liusu all had a bad feeling about the arrangement in the first place. However, it turned out to be rather beneficial for them; the only disadvantage was Feng Ge was difficult to get along with. They didn't even know her true identity until they asked a maid.

After meeting the three girls, gradually, Feng Ge grew to be almost fond of them. She even gave them several tips about cultivating.

Once Luo Zheng finally understood what had really happened in the past few days, he sighed and waved his hand gently.

Whoosh!

A piece of rice paper materialized in his hand.

"Fine. I have already shared one hundred and fifty words of the Truth Enlightenment with several people. In fact, if you had asked Qiu Yinhe or any of the other elders, you could've easily gotten it." Luo Zheng gently touched the scroll of rice paper, and it unfurled before him.

Then, with a slight gesture, Luo Zheng produced an ink brush in his other hand.

Swoosh! Swoosh! Swoosh!

With quick and vigorous movements, Luo Zheng wrote down one hundred and eighty words of the Truth Enlightenment.

"I've only deciphered the last thirty words recently. No one knows about it.

I'm giving this to you in exchange for teaching my girls," Luo Zheng said as he gently pushed the rice paper to fly towards Feng Ge.

Feng Ge had been indifferent about everything her whole life. Her sole interest was to improve her cultivation, so she didn't mind taking the time to give Nelly, Liusu and Xun some tips. It also seemed as though she'd taken a liking towards teaching.

Feng Ge snatched the paper eagerly, gulping down every word Luo Zheng wrote down.

After that, she carefully folded the rice paper. "Is there any more? If the Truth Enlightenment has been deciphered completely, it should be more than this."

Luo Zheng chuckled. There was no way he was going to give it all to her in one go.

"It's hard to decipher the rest. This is all." He paused, raising his eyebrow.

"If they can successfully break through to the Other Shore, I might be able to decipher something by then..."

Feng Ge knitted her brows, dubious as to whether Luo Zheng was speaking the truth. However, there was nothing she could do about it; in fact, she was already quite happy with the hundred and eighty words.

As Luo Zheng waved good-bye to Nelly, Liusu and Xun, he gently reminded them to stay safe. Then, he turned to leave the Fire Bearing Hall.

When Luo Zheng arrived at the door of the Fire Bearing Hall, he found Concubine Yan there waiting for him. There seemed to be a concerned look on her face, as though she wanted to tell him something, but she hesitated.

"Lady Concubine Yan," he said, "do you have something to tell me?"

Concubine Yan, noticing Luo Zheng had come out alone, realized that the three women would be staying at the Phoenix Palace.

"It is true that cultivating in the Phoenix Palace is far better than that in the Dragon City. If they stay here, they will be treated as direct descendants of the Taiyi Heaven Palace," said Concubine Yan cautiously.

"Thank you, Lady Concubine Yan," said Luo Zheng as he politely nodded his head.

Concubine Yan suddenly stretched out her hand and pressed a green jade token onto his palm. "This is the token of the Phoenix Palace. You must take it the next time you come here." Her eyes flashed seriously. "You were lucky to have entered the Primitive Illusion and emerged from it in one piece, but the next time, you won't be so lucky..."

Luo Zheng knew the guards would up their defense the next time he came here. If he were to visit the Phoenix Palace again, he knew that what Concubine Yan said was true.

Luo Zheng gratefully took the token and held it up to his eyes to observe it closely.

Without a word, he stuffed it into his space ring.

As Luo Zheng finally took his leave, Concubine Yan called to him softly, "If you have the time, you should come here more often..."

She smiled mischievously. Luo Zheng waved, but couldn't help but feel a bit uneasy.

He knew he was definitely going to come back sometime in the future, since the girls were cultivating here. But what did that have to do with Concubine Yan?

When Luo Zheng exited the gates of the Phoenix Palace, the dense, white mist of the Primitive Illusion ebbed away, giving him a path. The road before him seemed to be made of mist, soft as cotton.

After flying along the road for a while, Luo Zheng arrived at the Cloudy Mountain Palace on the hillside.

Luo Zheng was surprised to be met by three figures by a big stone that marked the front of the Cloudy Mountain Palace -- an old man in black flanked by two young men. While Luo Zheng had passed by the Cloudy Mountain Palace several times before, he'd never seen them before.
