Chapter 680 Yudie‘s Little Secret (Part Two)



"Do you know the cultivation levels of the others who attend it?" Yudie asked him again.

Zheng thought for a moment before shaking his head. "I really don't."

Yudie gave him a bitter smile, also shaking her head. "You don't even know how the others are situated, yet you plan on attending the Martial Arts Contest? The warriors who take part in the contest could be at any level below the late stage of the Internal Elixir Realm. They are elite disciples cultivated by old geezers of level four or five sects! Are you certain you can fight them? With you being at the third level of the Illuminating Soul Realm?

You are not strong like Blake, Benson, and some other disciples of Cloud Hall. You're not even in the running.

Moreover, the candidates attending the contest have already been chosen. If you wish to attend it, you have to attend it as an independent warrior and get the qualification by yourself.

If you like the idea of getting yourself killed in the process, I won't stop you," Yudie spouted out, her words a firm warning. Zheng grew silent.

"I must attend the Martial Arts Contest," he said as she looked at him coldly. "I must obtain the Dragon Soar Arena, and Marilyn must die!"

Making this announcement, Zheng turned around and left without bidding them goodbye.

"You!" Yudie had previously planned to warn Zheng not to attend the Martial Arts Contest, because she knew he would probably die otherwise. But Zheng's short speech had not given her any leeway to fully convince him.

However, the old lady, Yudie's master, smiled appreciatively. She had lived for long and was very experienced. She had been angry that Zheng blamed Yudie. Being a warrior, Zheng must know when to admit to his responsibilities.

"You are not allowed to leave!" Yudie outstretched her hand as she rebuilt the enchanted barrier. She had been back at the beginning level of the Virtual Tribulation Realm. It was easy for her to stop Zheng.

But at the same time, Zheng's body started to shake. He looked like a ghost; like a rippling figure under water. As he shook, he got past the enhanced barrier that Yudie had made with no scruples, as if it did not exist at all!

Yudie and the old lady could not believe their eyes. It seemed incredible!

They wondered if there was something wrong with their visions. What just happened?

One couldn't deny Yudie's cultivation base. She was at the beginning level of the Virtual Tribulation Realm!

Besides, she had sacrificed her cultivation strength nine times now, and her power was far greater compared to ordinary warriors who were at the beginning level of the same realm. Her enchanted barrier was exceptionally strong, but just now Zheng passed it so smoothly, as if it wasn't present before him at all. What on earth did he just do to surpass that?

"If my eyes serve me correctly, Zheng seemed to utilize the strength of the Space Law. Also... he seems to have learned that Law quite well. Even Saint Morphens, is not able to perform as well as Zheng," the old lady said, recovering first. She shook her head disbelievingly.

Morphens had learned the Space Rules like few others could. However, he could use his Mystical Pace in the weak space, which was entirely different from what Zheng did. Zheng passed Tia's enchanted barrier with ease. It seemed that Zheng didn't do anything fancy, but his moving skills themselves contained a profound understanding of the Space Rule.

Yudie wanted to catch up to Zheng. But the old lady, her mentor, was still here. She hesitated, trying to hold herself back.

"That young boy... is unusual. If he insists on attending the Martial Arts Contest, just let him go," the old lady said calmly. She threw Yudie a meaningful look. "Which brings me to my next question. Tell me, why did you stop him from attending the Martial Arts Contest? I'm curious."

Yudie blinked, answering, "No matter what Zheng has done, he is a disciple of Cloud Hall. I would feel tremendously guilty if he is killed!"

"Is that right? If so, then what about the others? Aren't Blake, Benson, and the others disciples of our Cloud Hall too? If he feared death, he shouldn't have started to cultivate the martial arts in the beginning." A smile lingered in the old lay's opalescent eyes as she finished her words.

"I don't know what you mean, master," Yudie spluttered, still acting the naive little girl.

The old lady sighed, her expression becoming grave. She had lived for so long. How could she not see what was blooming between Yudie and Zheng?

The way Yudie talked to Zheng was so different from how she reacted to others. She did not behave like she was the rightful head of the Cloud Hall at all! How could her master not put two and two together then? She knew the secret Yudie was harboring.

The old lady knew that Yudie and Zheng had gone through something together at the Cloud Road, however, she did not know exactly what had occurred. It did not matter, in any case. As long as Zheng continued to have breakthroughs and could be powerful enough, she wouldn't mind marrying Tia off to Zheng.

As per her point of view, Zheng truly was a rare genius. His potential was as great as Tia's. For the sake of his cultivation, using up all the resources at Cloud Hall would be worth it.

It was a pity that Zheng was too weak right now. Not all geniuses got the chance to grow without a spanner in the works. Zheng expected to kill Marilyn and declared a war against Eddie Cui in the process. It wasn't certain that Zheng would survive the Martial Arts Contest at all.
